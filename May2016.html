DIVERSITY + INCLUSION

What we're doing + next steps

DeLisa Alexander, EVP + Chief People Officer, gave an update on our diversity and inclusion roadmap, including what we’ve accomplished and the next steps to advance our strategy.

What we're doing

  • Published first draft of Diversity + Inclusion Strategy
  • 1,100+ associates viewed the draft and we received 150+ emails and comments with feedback
  • Summary of feedback and updated strategy will be published in Q2
  • Provided external benchmarks, internal data, insights, and opportunities to senior leaders for consideration in this year’s goals
  • Began developing inventory of existing diversity and inclusion solutions for managers and associates (will be published on Mojo in Q2)
  • Attended Clayman Institute corporate partners meeting

Next steps

  • Define timeline, plan, and approach on process audit (including pay equity project)
  • Develop governance, funding model, and toolkits to support diversity communities of passion
  • Enhance existing diversity and inclusion offerings and conduct needs assessment to identify additional training, programs, and tools
  • Update recruiting tools to support diverse talent pools

Managing diversity dynamics

Caroline Simard, PhD – Sr. Director Research, Stanford University's Clayman Institute; Exponential Talent LLC

Caroline's presentation taught us:

  • The different dimensions of diversity
  • Why managing diverse teams is a major leadership challenge
  • What dynamics and tools can help unlock the innovation potential of diverse teams
  • How diversity and inclusion impact meritocracy and Red Hat’s culture
  • How unconscious bias and stereotypes impact team dynamics, talent decisions, and our ability to have productive debates

> View slides from the presentation: https://mojo.redhat.com/docs/DOC-1079277

What works at Red Hat

Chris Wright, VP + Chief Technologist, Mike Hideo-Smith, Senior Director, Project Management, Phil Andrews, VP, Business Development, and Mary Oakley, Director, Regional Sales shared their experiences and insight into:
