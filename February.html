Red Hat's strategy story

Joe Anglim, Director of Corporate Strategy, discussed Red Hat's framework, our company and market context, and our challenges and next steps.

  • Achieving Red Hat's vision is a journey with a number of strategic phases.
  • Our sources of advantage are defensible and relevant, but there are still a number of challenges and considerations.
  • Our strategic framework strongly influences Red Hat's annual planning activities, but needs to be refreshed every 3-5 years.

Connecting Red Hat's strategy to goals

DeLisa Alexander, EVP + Chief People Officer, discussed combining our strategic capabilities and our talent management and development capabilities.

  • A big part of our FY17 goals is to make sure associates understand the strategy and increase their abilities to contribute at Red Hat.
  • Red Hat's strategic framework influences our goals and initiatives. Make sure you have conversations with your associates on how their work connects to our strategy.

Connecting your team to the strategy

TEAM COMMUNICATIONS: Tim Burke, Vice President - Linux, Cloud, Storage Engineering

  • Teams want to know their place in the stack.
  • Accept responsibility to talk to your team, don't just forward a link.
  • Boil it down to 3-5 main themes, and what's most pertinent for your team.
  • Start with corporate context, then discuss your team's successes, trends, and challenges and how this relates to the strategy.
  • Make sure your team knows why what they do matters.

TRANSFORMING THE STRATEGY INTO REALITY WITHIN SUPPORT DELIVERY: Maria DiMaio, Senior Manager – Customer Experience + Engagement

  • Develop and empower thought leaders as evangelists. This enhances the influence competency in associates and increases a shared purpose beyond your team.
  • Accept responsibility to talk to your team, don't just forward a link.
  • Reward progress and initiative while cultivating our unique culture. This strengthens key behavior of the Red Hat Multiplier and ignites innovation.
